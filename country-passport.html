About Passport Services in Nassau County

Nassau County offers numerous passport acceptance facilities throughout the county, including post offices, libraries, and county clerk offices serving Long Island residents. Whether you need to apply for a first-time passport, renew an existing passport, or obtain a passport for your child, you'll find authorized passport acceptance facilities throughout the county.

Nassau County passport facilities include USPS post offices, public libraries, county clerk offices, and city halls. Most facilities accept walk-ins, but we strongly recommend calling ahead to confirm passport service hours and to schedule an appointment to minimize your wait time.

💡 Before You Visit

Make sure you have all required documents before visiting a passport acceptance facility. Most locations accept walk-ins, but calling ahead is recommended.

  • • Bring proof of U.S. citizenship (certified birth certificate or previous passport)
  • • Bring valid photo ID (driver's license or state ID)
  • • Do NOT sign Form DS-11 until you're at the facility
  • • Both parents must be present for child passport applications

Which Expedited Option Should You Choose?
  • New York Passport Agency: Best for urgent travel within 14 days. Requires proof of travel and in-person appointment in Manhattan. Most affordable expedited option.
  • National Passport Service: Best if you can't get an agency appointment or want door-to-door service from Nassau County. No travel proof required. More expensive but most convenient.
  • Regular Expedited ($60): Best if you have 2-3 weeks. Apply at any Nassau County passport facility listed above and add expedited service.

Tips for Visiting Passport Facilities in Nassau County

Best Times to Visit

Passport acceptance facilities in Nassau County can get busy, especially during peak travel seasons (summer and holidays). Generally, mid-morning on Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday offers the shortest wait times. Avoid Monday mornings, lunch hours (12-2 PM), and the weeks before major holidays when passport applications spike.

Required Documents

For Adult First-Time Applicants

  • • Proof of U.S. citizenship (certified birth certificate)
  • • Valid photo ID (driver's license or state ID)
  • • One passport photo (2x2 inches)
  • • Form DS-11 (completed but NOT signed)
  • • Payment ($130 application fee + $35 execution fee)

For Child Passport (Under 16)

  • • Child's birth certificate (original or certified copy)
  • • Both parents must appear with child
  • • Parents' photo IDs
  • • Child's passport photo
  • • Payment ($100 application fee + $35 execution fee)

Processing Times & Fees

Routine processing takes 4-6 weeks from when the Department of State receives your application. Expedited processing (2-3 weeks) is available for an additional $60 fee. The execution fee ($35) is paid at the acceptance facility, while the application fee is paid separately by check or money order to "U.S. Department of State."

⚠️ Important Reminder

Passport service hours and availability are subject to change. We recommend calling your chosen passport acceptance facility before your visit to confirm current hours, photo service availability, and any temporary closures or special requirements. Do NOT sign Form DS-11 until you're at the facility.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a passport cost in Nassau County?

A first-time adult passport book costs $165 total ($130 application fee + $35 execution fee). Child passports (under 16) cost $135 total ($100 + $35). Passport photos cost an additional $15 at most facilities. Expedited processing adds $60.

Do I need an appointment for a passport in Nassau County?

Most passport acceptance facilities in Nassau County accept walk-ins, but some require or recommend appointments. It's best to call ahead to your chosen location to confirm their appointment policy and passport service hours.

What is the closest passport office to me in Nassau County?

Nassau County has 12 passport acceptance facilities throughout the county. The closest one depends on your location. Use the list above to find facilities in Garden City, Hempstead, Mineola, Long Beach, Rockville Centre, and other towns.

How long does it take to get a passport in Nassau County?

Routine processing takes 4-6 weeks from when the State Department receives your application (add 5-7 days for mailing). Expedited processing takes 2-3 weeks for an additional $60 fee. For urgent travel (within 14 days), contact the New York Passport Agency.

Can I get same-day passport photos in Nassau County?

Yes, most USPS post offices and the Nassau County Clerk office offer passport photo services on-site for approximately $15. Many facilities can take your photo the same day you apply. Call ahead to confirm photo availability.

What documents do I need to bring to apply for a passport?

You need: (1) Proof of U.S. citizenship (birth certificate or previous passport), (2) Valid photo ID (driver's license), (3) One passport photo, (4) Completed Form DS-11 (unsigned), and (5) Payment ($130-165 depending on type). Both parents must appear for child applications.
